# Search Result Evaluation Examples

These examples demonstrate how to evaluate search results based on **user intent**, **relevance**, and **helpfulness**.

---

## Example 1

**Query:** `best beginner guitar`  
**User Intent:** Informational — user wants recommendations for beginner guitars and buying advice.

### Results Evaluation

**Result 1:** “Top 10 Beginner Guitars (2026 Buyer’s Guide)”  
- **Rating:** Highly Relevant  
- **Why:** Directly matches intent; includes comparison criteria, price ranges, and updated recommendations.

**Result 2:** Retail listing for a single beginner guitar  
- **Rating:** Moderately Relevant  
- **Why:** Relevant product, but narrow; lacks broad recommendations and learning context.

**Result 3:** Wikipedia article: “Guitar”  
- **Rating:** Slightly Relevant  
- **Why:** Topic-related but does not help the user choose a beginner guitar.

### Overall SERP Quality
**High** — multiple results satisfy the intent; at least one result strongly helps the user complete the task.

---

## Example 2

**Query:** `how to reset iphone password`  
**User Intent:** Help / troubleshooting — user needs clear steps to regain access safely.

### Results Evaluation

**Result 1:** Official support article with step-by-step recovery  
- **Rating:** Highly Relevant  
- **Why:** Authoritative and procedural; minimizes risk of bad advice.

**Result 2:** Forum post with mixed suggestions  
- **Rating:** Slightly Relevant  
- **Why:** Might contain helpful tips, but quality is inconsistent and could be unsafe/misleading.

**Result 3:** General article about iPhone security  
- **Rating:** Moderately Relevant  
- **Why:** Helpful background, but not directly actionable for the user’s immediate need.

### Overall SERP Quality
**Medium to High** — intent is met if authoritative guidance appears prominently.
